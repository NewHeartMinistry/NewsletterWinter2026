"The Son of Man came not to be served but to serve..." â€” Mark 10:45

In the fall of 2024, I entered New Heart without really knowing what the program was about, at my wife's suggestion, bringing only the bare minimum. As a new believer baptized less than a year ago, I shared a room with two pastors and witnessed up close how faith is revealed through life rather than words. Their attitude and service were my first lesson in learning what faith truly means through experience, and that experience remained deep in my heart for a long time.

Having been in a position of receiving service, I naturally felt compelled to stand again in that place as someone who serves. The grace I received was too clear. However, at the same time, there was fear. I felt my faith was still small, and I worried about becoming a burden to other team members. Nevertheless, remembering the inspiration and grace I had received, I returned to New Heart hoping that love would be conveyed to other candidates as well.

When I was a participant, I felt Jesus through the team members who served. I saw His love in their quiet movements without drawing attention to themselves. Conversely, looking at the candidates while serving as a team member, I saw another face of Jesus. I saw the Lord still at work in their opening hearts, receiving grace, and praising.

At this New Heart, I served on the coordination team, helping with the overall flow of the program. It was work managing schedules and logistics, taking responsibility for invisible parts. The most rewarding moments were when the candidates' expressions changed. Every time I saw hearts that had been hardened soften and become free in worship, I felt that all this labor was never in vain.

Of course, it wasn't easy. Being my first time serving, I moved at an overpace with excessive enthusiasm, and when my physical strength declined, my heart collapsed along with it. Each time, I recalled the moments of gratitude and grace I had received in the past, and the faces of candidates experiencing grace right before me lifted me up again. Watching God's hand guide everything smoothly despite inadequate preparation and unexpected situations, I clearly felt that the Lord is still alive.

Through this service, God gave me a clear message. It wasn't about years of faith or depth of spirituality, but the fact that God uses those who willingly humble themselves. The truth that the highest One served in the lowest place planted a completely new value system in me, who had lived hoping to rise high in the world. It was a time to learn through my body that being lowly is not shabby at all, but rather the most beautiful place.

What's interesting is that I didn't feel much change in the moment of serving. I thought I was serving out of grace, but in fact, my "desire to do well" and "my own zeal" were ahead. As time passed and I meditated, I realized how worldly last year's service had been. I easily became exhausted because I tried to please the Lord with my own righteousness and effort.

In that realization, I learned again what true worship and ministry are. My position, environment, and circumstances had nothing to do with the essence of service. True worship was dying to myself in the world and turning only toward the Lord. Even short moments of whole-hearted praise and prayer became times of even deeper grace.

The foot washing ceremony especially remains long in my memory. In the tears shed as family, team members, and candidates washed each other's feet, I saw how the Lord loved His disciples and how that love should continue into further service. God's way of healing wounded hearts and giving strength to serve again was always love.

If I were to express this service in one word, it would be "prostration." I learned that the grace of serving is far greater than the grace received. Even if it seems small in the world, if it's something the Lord is pleased with, that is enough.

I am still someone who hasn't been walking the path of faith for very long. Sometimes it's slow and I become impatient. However, the Lord tells me not to overdo it, that He will bear the yoke together with His beloved son. I believe this journey of service is ultimately not a path I walk alone, but a path I walk with the Lord.

I offer gratitude and praise to the Lord who called me to this place again. I hope to continue serving willingly with humility in all the places of ministry the Lord will lead.
